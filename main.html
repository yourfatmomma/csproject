Standard & Poor's 500

Commonly known as the S&P 500, is a stock market index that tracks the performance of 500 large companies listed on stock exchanges in the United States. It is widely regarded as one of the best representations of the U.S. stock market and a leading indicator of the nation's economic health.

The S&P 500 is maintained by S&P Dow Jones Indices and is used as a benchmark for U.S. stock market performance. Its composition is designed to reflect the overall health and growth of the U.S. economy, making it a crucial indicator for investors, policymakers, and economists alike.
